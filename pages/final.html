Final Composition Assignment by Dor Rondel

Part 1 - Simple Variations on a Theme

Composer's Notes

Musical composition tasks that are loosely defined such as this one, typically turn out to be creative type of assignments. By allowing for a lot of flexibility (such as choosing my own theme), the final results almost always turn out unique, and if enough thought was put into them, auditorily pleasing as well. Reading through the assignment prior to starting it, I noticed that it uses the C scale. Therefore, the only notes selected for the composition were {C, E, G}. Aside from that, it seemed as if the hat operator for concurrent play would not work in SonicText mode. I found this after reading the terminal output, which read: Exception in thread "Clay Thread" java.lang.ClassCastException: note.SonicTextNote cannot be cast to note. SonicNote". This prompted me to use Sonic mode, which allowed all the concurrently played compositons to be audible. Additionally, I tried playing with tempo for a bit, and it would seem that Clay cannot produce sounds with a shorter duration than an eigth note.

Part 2 - Structural Refinement Grammar Algorithmic Composition

Composer's Notes

Structural refinement grammars provide an additional layer of abstraction for the various processes of musical composition, sitting on top of L-systems and context free grammars. The hardest part of the task was devising a grammar that would be flexible enough to allow for high output diversity and musical quality. That being said, I stuck to the C-minor scale for this one, using only {C, E, G} notes. To increase unpredictablity and variability I included various different mappings and tempos within the grammar. The derivations weren't hard - just very tedious.

Part 3 - Minimalist Madness

Composer's Notes

The tune I chose to create is named Amplitude. The name was chosen as the main theme of the piece is the constant change in amplitude between subsuquent notes. Amplitude is a minimal piece because it adheres to most of the minimal music characteristics. It's limited in musical materials, as it is played on a single instrument. It has an experimental flavor, as it doesn't abide to any scale or taught recommendations. The approach to creating the piece focused on the fact that humans like predictability, and what most would consider good music, would be predictable to a degree. The transformation is gradual, rising one note per iteration. The subsequent not is based on additive/subtractive measure. And there is a strict rule as to how much you can rise and fall per given iteration. In making the piece, the hardest part was coming up with the idea of what to make. After coming up with it, it was a pretty simple implentation. Basically keep doubling the amount you lower the pitch by the initial factor you raised it by, and raise it at the end by the same initial amount to return to the starting note. Repeat that 10 times. Then to fill up the time and make the piece more interesting, follow the same note alteration process for the tempo, and voilà, you have Alteration.

Part 4 - Fractured Familiar Tune

Composer's Notes

For this portion of the assignment, I selected to modify Bach's Invention 13. I selected this tune as it was the only famous tune I found with a partially JFugue MusicString scribed online, as i do not now how to read sheet music. The source can be found here. The original piece, uses sixteenth and eighth notes, for the part I chose to alter. This was a problem, as MxM has issues with producing notes of that duration. Therefore, the first modification made was slowing the tune down by a factor of 4. After that, I produced a variation of the tune, one octave lower. Finally, the final version of the tune is the two aformentioned tunes played in organum.
